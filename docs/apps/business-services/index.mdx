## Overview

Business Services let you build custom maps of your business-critical functions. Unlike [Application Groups](/apps/application-groups/index.mdx), which are automatically generated from discovered connections and update dynamically as new data is found, Business Services are static and designed entirely by you. They provide a stable, referenceable representation of your applications that only changes when you edit them manually or configure [alerts](#alerts) to respond to dependency changes. Business Services can include any combination of devices, resources, application components, and other Business Services that you identify as critical to your operations.

Building Business Services is typically the final step in the [Application Dependency Mapping](/apps/enterprise-application-dependency-mapping/index.mdx) workflow: after discovery has populated your devices, services, and Application Groups, you assemble them into Business Services that represent how your organization delivers value.

Use Business Services to:

- Create services from scratch or from existing Application Groups.
- Relate Business Services to other Business Services, breaking large services into smaller, reusable components.
- Add and remove devices, resources, and application components.
- Customize the layout, connections, and labels on the visualization canvas.
- Migrate Business Services to third-party platforms like Microsoft Azure.

Navigate to **Applications > Business Services** from the Device42 menu to display the Business Services list page.

## Create a Business Service

From the Business Services list page, click **Create** to define a new Business Service, or clone an existing one to use as a starting point. See [Clone a Business Service](#clone-a-business-service).

To create a new Business Service, fill out the **Info** form. After saving, you'll have access to the **Application Layout** canvas to visualize dependencies and relationships between items.

The **Info** form includes the following fields:

- Enter the application **Name** and select the **Service Type** (COTS, COTS with Customization, or Custom).

### Add and Remove Items

From the **Info** form, you can associate the following items with your Business Service (in addition to File Attachments and Custom Fields):

- **Business Services:** Link other Business Services to model service-to-service relationships. For example, a "Human Resources" Business Service might include "Payroll," "Benefits," and "PTO" as associated Business Services.
- **Devices:** Add the physical or virtual machines that support the service.
- **Resources:** Add cloud or infrastructure resources such as databases and storage.
- **Application Components:** Add the application layers (web servers, databases, load balancers) that make up the service.

Currently, Application Groups can only be added from the canvas. See [Add Application Groups](#add-application-groups) below.

### Add Business Services

Adding a Business Service to another Business Service lets you model how higher-level services depend on smaller, reusable services. For example, a company-wide "Human Resources" service might reference separate "Payroll," "Benefits," and "PTO" Business Services, each with their own devices and application components. This makes it easier to maintain and understand complex service hierarchies without duplicating infrastructure details across multiple Business Services.

Drag the **Business Service** block to the canvas to open the **Add Business Service** box.

Search for a Business Service by typing its name in the search box. You can also click the **+** button to add a new Business Service.

### Add Application Groups

[Application Groups](/apps/application-groups/index.mdx) are automatically generated sets of devices and services that share discovered communication dependencies. Adding an Application Group to a Business Service brings in its discovered infrastructure, so you don't need to manually add each device. As discovery finds new dependencies, the Application Group updates automatically.

You can also configure [alerts](#alerts) to receive notifications when devices are added to or removed from an Application Group linked to your Business Service.

Drag an **Application Group** block to the canvas to open the **Add Application Group** dialog. Search for a group by name, or click the **+** button to create a new one.

## Alerts

:::note
To continue receiving alerts for existing Business Services, edit each Business Service and add the relevant Application Group (previously known as "Affinity Group").
:::

You can set up alerts to receive notifications when Application Group dependencies are added to or removed from a Business Service.

To create an alert, select **Analytics > Setup Alerts** from the Device42 main menu and click **Create**.

<ThemedImage
  alt="Setup Alert menu"
  sources={{
    light: useBaseUrl('/assets/images/business-services/alerts-menu-light.png'),
    dark: useBaseUrl('/assets/images/business-services/alerts-menu-dark.png'),
  }}
/>


- Enter a **Name** for the alert and select **Business Service** as the **Rule Type**.
- For the **Trigger**, select **New Application Group Dependency Found** or **Application Group Dependency Removed**.
- Select the **Alert Type**.
    - If you select **D42 Notification**, choose an **Escalation Profile** to configure email notification tiers. Manage Escalation Profiles at **Tools > Admins & Permissions > Escalation Profiles**.


<ThemedImage
  alt="Business Service alert"
  sources={{
    light: useBaseUrl('/assets/images/business-services/add-alert-light.png'),
    dark: useBaseUrl('/assets/images/business-services/add-alert-dark.png'),
  }}
/>

As you configure the alert, Device42 displays the alert definition based on your selections. For example:

> When the individual Business Service Jupiter service app has reported New Application Group Dependency Found then send notification to users assigned to Main profile.

### Auto Apply Changes

Check the **Auto Apply changes** box to automatically add or remove connections when Application Group dependencies change. You will still receive a notification for each change.

The **Auto Apply changes** option is unchecked by default. When disabled, you receive a notification with the option to manually add or remove the connection.

### Apply to Selected Objects

Depending on the **Rule Type**, you may see an **Apply To** option. Use the **Available objects** list on the left to select objects and move them to the **Chosen objects** list on the right.

## Edit the Visualization

You can modify objects and connections directly on the canvas.

### Delete an Object

To delete a device or other object on the canvas, select it and press the **Delete** key, or right-click and select **Delete** from the context menu.

![](/assets/images/WEB-542_BusinessApps-Canvas-Ex-3-PopupMenu.png)

:::note
Removing an object does not remove its connections on the canvas or affect data stored in Application Groups.
:::

### Rename an Object

To rename an object on the canvas, select it, double-click the name, and type a new name.

:::note
The renamed label is only for display in this Business Service visualization. It does not change the underlying record.
:::

### Add or Edit Connections

1. Hover over an object to display its connection points (green dots). Click a green dot and drag to connect it to another object.

   ![](/assets/images/WEB-542_BusinessApps-Canvas-Ex-4-ConnectorPoint.png)

2. To delete a connection, select the connection and press the **Delete** key, or right-click and select **Delete** from the context menu.

:::note
Deleting a connection does not affect the calculated information stored in Application Groups.
:::

### Export to Microsoft Azure Migrate (Cloud Migrations)

Device42 can export one or more of your Business Services directly to the [Microsoft Azure](https://portal.azure.com/#home) cloud platform via [Azure Migrate](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/products/azure-migrate/).

For detailed instructions, see the [Azure Migrate integration page](/integration/external-integrations/azure-migrate.mdx).

### Export to RiverMeadow 

Click the **Export** button to export a CSV file with the IP address(es) and credentials for the server instance(s) that power your chosen Business Services.

Save the exported CSV and then open it using RiverMeadow to perform your migration.

:::tip
Support for additional migration platforms is being added. Contact [support@device42.com](mailto:support@device42.com) with questions.
:::
